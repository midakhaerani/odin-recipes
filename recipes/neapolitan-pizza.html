Neapolitan Pizza Recipe

Description

Neapolitan pizza is a classic Italian pizza known for its soft, airy crust, thin center, and simple, high-quality ingredients. Originating from Naples, it is traditionally baked at very high temperatures, which creates a lightly charred exterior while keeping the inside tender and moist.

This pizza focuses on balance and restraint: a naturally fermented dough, bright San Marzano tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, fragrant basil, and extra-virgin olive oil. When done correctly, each bite highlights the flavor of the dough as much as the toppings.

Ingredients

  • Tipo 00 flour
  • Water
  • Sea salt
  • Fresh yeast or instant dry yeast
  • San Marzano tomatoes (crushed)
  • Fresh mozzarella (preferably mozzarella di bufala)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Mix water and yeast until dissolved, then gradually add flour and salt to form a soft dough.
  2. Knead the dough until smooth and elastic, then let it rest and ferment at room temperature until doubled in size.
  3. Divide the dough into individual balls and allow them to rest again until relaxed and airy.
  4. Crush the tomatoes by hand and season lightly with salt.
  5. Stretch each dough ball gently into a thin round, leaving a thicker edge for the crust.
  6. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough, add sliced mozzarella, and drizzle with olive oil.
  7. Bake in a very hot oven or pizza oven until the crust puffs, blisters, and develops light charring.
  8. Remove from the oven, garnish with fresh basil, and serve immediately.
